## 1. Estimation

**Key ideas**

- Point estimator: sample statistic (e.g. $\bar{x}$) estimates population parameter (e.g. $\mu$).
- CI = point estimate $\pm$ margin of error.

> [!formula] **Confidence intervals**
> 
> - If $\sigma$ known (large $n$):  
>     $$\bar{x}\pm z_{\alpha/2}\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}$$
> - If $\sigma$ unknown (small $n$):  
>     $$\bar{x}\pm t_{\alpha/2,,n-1}\frac{s}{\sqrt{n}}$$
> - Proportion CI:  
>     $$\hat p\pm z_{\alpha/2}\sqrt{\frac{\hat p(1-\hat p)}{n}}$$

> [!example] **Confidence interval example**  
> $n=25,\ \bar x=50,\ s=4,\ 95%$ CI: $t_{0.025,24}\approx2.064$, SE $=4/5=0.8$.  
> CI: $50\pm2.064(0.8)=(48.35,\ 51.65)$

## 2. Test of Significance

**Key ideas**

- State $H_0$ and $H_1$, pick $\alpha$, compute test statistic, compare with critical or p-value.

> [!formula] **Test statistics**
> 
> - Known $\sigma$:  
>     $$z=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}}$$
> - Unknown $\sigma$:  
>     $$t=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{s/\sqrt{n}}$$

> [!example] **Example**  
> With $n=25,\bar x=50,s=4$: test $H_0:\mu=50$ at $\alpha=0.05$.  
> $t=0$, so fail to reject $H_0$.

## 3. Regression & Correlation

**Key ideas**

- Pearson $r$ measures linear association.
- Simple linear regression: $y=\beta_0+\beta_1x+\varepsilon$.

> [!formula] **Least squares estimators**  
> $$\hat\beta_1=\frac{\sum (x_i-\bar x)(y_i-\bar y)}{\sum (x_i-\bar x)^2}$$  
> $$\hat\beta_0=\bar y-\hat\beta_1\bar x$$  
> $$R^2=r^2$$

> [!example] **Example**  
> $x=[1,2,3,4],\ y=[2,4,5,7]$  
> $\Rightarrow\ \hat\beta_1=1.6,\ \hat\beta_0=0.5$  
> Regression line: $\hat y=0.5+1.6x$

## 4. Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)

### One-Way ANOVA

**Idea:** Compare $k$ group means by splitting total variance.

> [!formula] **Formulas**  
> $$SS_T=SS_B+SS_W$$  
> $$MS_B=\frac{SS_B}{k-1},\quad MS_W=\frac{SS_W}{N-k}$$  
> $$F=\frac{MS_B}{MS_W}$$  
> Reject $H_0$ if $F>F_{\alpha,k-1,N-k}$.

> [!example] **Example**  
> Groups: A(5,7,9), B(10,12,14), C(20,22,24).  
> $\bar X_{total}=13.67$. Compute SS and find $F\gg F_{0.05,2,6}$ → reject $H_0$.

---

### Two-Way ANOVA (No Interaction)

**Model:**

$$Y_{ij}=\mu+\alpha_i+\beta_j+\varepsilon_{ij}$$  
Test main effects:  
$$F_A=\frac{MS_A}{MS_E},\quad F_B=\frac{MS_B}{MS_E}$$
 
 
  
Assumptions: independence, normality, equal variances.

**Links**  
- [One-way ANOVA guide (Scribbr)](https://www.scribbr.com/statistics/one-way-anova/)

## 5. Formula Sheet

> [!formula] **Summary**
> - CI (unknown $\sigma$): $\bar x\pm t_{\alpha/2,n-1}\frac{s}{\sqrt n}$  
> - $z$-test: $z=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt n}$  
> - $t$-test: $t=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{s/\sqrt n}$  
> - Regression slope: $\hat\beta_1=\frac{\sum (x-\bar x)(y-\bar y)}{\sum (x-\bar x)^2}$  
> - ANOVA $F$-ratio: $F=\frac{MS_B}{MS_W}$
